The deal you can't see

The dealership isn't built for you. It's built around you.

The negotiation games. The "let me check with my manager" loops. The trade-in shuffle — $1,000 more on your trade, then $1,500 buried somewhere else. The Saturday that drags into evening on purpose.

On used cars: same playbook, different vocabulary. "We just took it in trade." "Carfax shows clean." "CPO-certified." Ted reads them all.

Then comes the finance office. Different person, different desk, different math. Interest rate markups. Inflated warranties. Add-ons you never asked for. A few hundred here, a couple thousand there. That's where the dealership actually makes its money.

Most car-buying services hand you off at the showroom door and wave goodbye. Ted walks you through both rooms.

About Ted

Fifty years. Every side of the deal.

For nearly fifty years, Ted has worked every side of the auto industry. He's owned gas stations. Run multi-line dealerships. Managed import and domestic showrooms. He's run the sales floor and the finance desk both — which means he knows how the price gets set, how the financing really works, and where the dealership's leverage lives. Because he used to be the one holding it.

These days he buys and sells at dealer auctions — which means his clients get access to wholesale inventory and pricing that retail buyers never see.

When he's not negotiating, Ted's on the water. He's based in New Hampshire and works remotely with clients across the country.

Common questions

What people ask before their first call.

Be honest — will I save more than your fee?

Most clients do, often by a multiple. Ted only takes on clients he's confident he can help. On your free 15-minute call, he'll tell you whether he can save you more than his fee. If he can't, he'll say so — and point you to a better path. He'd rather lose a client than take one he can't help.

What if you can't save me more than I pay you?

If Ted takes you on and the savings don't come through, he'll make it right. The math has to work in your favor — that's the whole point. Specifics are discussed before any money changes hands, on your free consult.

Do you work with used cars and trade-ins?

Yes — both, and used is often where Ted saves clients the most. He can read auction history, spot a flipped or rebadged car, and tell you which "Certified Pre-Owned" programs are real versus marketing. He's also licensed to buy and sell at dealer auctions, which means he can source vehicles before they hit a retail lot, at wholesale prices. On trade-ins, he negotiates them separately from the purchase price — so the dealer can't bury one in the other (which is exactly what they try to do).

How does this work if you're not in my state?

Ted negotiates remotely with dealers across the country. Most of the work happens over the phone and email — pricing, trade-in, financing terms — long before you walk in to pick up the keys. He's based in New Hampshire and works with clients across the U.S.

What states do you work in?

Ted is licensed in New Hampshire and works remotely with clients across the U.S. as their personal advisor — the dealer remains the licensed party in every transaction. On your free 15-minute call, Ted will confirm anything specific to your state.

Will the dealer even talk to you?

Yes. Ted spent decades on the dealer side — he knows how their week is structured, who has authority on what, and how to frame an offer that lands. Most dealers prefer working with him because he comes to the table prepared and serious.

What if I've already picked a car?

That's what the Negotiation level is for. You bring Ted the car, he handles the rest — pricing, trade-in, financing review, and the finance office walk-through.

Is the consultation really free?

Yes. The first 15-minute call is on Ted — it's how he figures out if he's the right fit, and how you figure out if you trust him. No pressure, no obligation, no pitch.

How are you different from Costco Auto, TrueCar, or CarEdge?

Those services give you a referral to a participating dealer with a pre-negotiated price. That's a fine baseline — but it's still a dealer-friendly number, and you're on your own once you walk into the finance office. Ted is your representative, not the dealer's. He runs both halves of the deal — including the finance office, where most of the damage happens.
